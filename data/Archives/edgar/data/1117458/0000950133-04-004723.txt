<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SECTION 3 &#151; SECURITIES AND TRADING MARKETS</B>






<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ITEM 3.01 &#151; Notice of Delisting or Failure to Satisfy a Continued Listing Rule or Standard;
Transfer of Listing</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;16, 2004, O2Diesel Corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;) received notice from the staff of
The American Stock Exchange (&#147;AMEX&#148;) indicating that the Company is not in compliance with Section
1003(a)(i) of the AMEX Company Guide, in that its stockholders&#146; equity is less than $2&nbsp;million and
it has sustained losses from continuing operations and/or net losses in two of its three most
recent fiscal years, and with Section&nbsp;1003(a)(ii) of the AMEX Company Guide, in that its
stockholders&#146; equity is less than $4&nbsp;million and it has sustained losses from continuing operations
and/or net losses in three of its four most recent fiscal years.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In order to maintain listing of the Company&#146;s Common Stock on AMEX, the Company must submit a
plan by January&nbsp;18, 2005, advising AMEX of the actions the Company has taken, or will take, that
would bring it into compliance with the applicable listing standards. If AMEX accepts the plan,
the Company may be able to continue its listing during the plan period of up to 18&nbsp;months, during
which time the Company will be subject to periodic review to determine whether it is making
progress consistent with the plan. If AMEX does not accept the Company&#146;s plan, or even if
accepted, the Company is not in compliance with the continued listing standards at the end of the
18&nbsp;month period or does not make progress consistent with the plan during such period, AMEX may
initiate delisting proceedings with respect to the Common Stock.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company intends to submit a compliance plan to AMEX by January&nbsp;18, 2005. The Company&#146;s
Common Stock continues to trade on AMEX.
